# üåê Miriam Gradel ‚Äî Journalism & Communication Portfolio

A modern, content-driven website built for a journalist, communication strategist, and multimedia storyteller.

## üìñ Overview

**Miriam Gradel** is a professional journalism and communication portfolio designed to showcase written work, audio production, video journalism, and communication services.
The platform is built as a **dynamic editorial hub**, where visitors can browse articles, explore multimedia content, learn about communications services, and contact Miriam through an integrated Work-With-Me section.
The website also includes a **behind-the-scenes journal**, a mobile-first navigation system, immersive page animations, and a fully custom visual identity.
This platform is built using **Django + Wagtail**, providing fast performance, editor-friendly content management, and a highly extendable architecture suitable for ongoing content publication.

# User Experience (UX)

## Ideal User

The website is designed for a broad professional audience, including:

- Editors, journalists, and media commissioners reviewing Miriam‚Äôs work  
- Readers exploring her written, audio, or video journalism  
- Organisations and clients seeking communication strategy or consulting  
- Professionals looking for clear examples of storytelling, media production, or editorial expertise  
- Individuals interested in behind-the-scenes insights into reporting and content creation  
- International audiences, supported by multilingual capabilities  

Visitors to the **Miriam Gradel** website typically want to:

- Discover **journalistic work** in multiple formats (written, audio, video)  
- Access **clear, well-organized portfolios** of stories, reports, and multimedia projects  
- Learn about **communication services**, including consulting and media strategy  
- Contact Miriam through a streamlined **Work-With-Me** process  
- Enjoy a clean, intuitive, highly readable **editorial interface**  
- Explore behind-the-scenes content, process notes, and updates through the BTS section  

## Why the Website Serves These Needs Effectively

- **Intuitive navigation** allows visitors to quickly browse by medium: Written, Audio, or Video.  
- A flexible CMS (Wagtail) ensures each journalism format is presented with the appropriate layout, typography, and embedded media.
- The **Communication** and **Work-With-Me** sections guide prospective clients through services and contact pathways, supported by a consistent design and clear calls-to-action.
- The website uses **editorial-grade design patterns** ‚Äî spacious layouts, strong readability, custom ribbons, and responsive typography ‚Äî to deliver a premium reading experience.
- Integrated **multimedia support** ensures video, audio, and embeds load smoothly and responsively across devices.
- The **Behind the Scenes** area gives readers an informal space to explore process insights, updates, and personal notes. 
- Multilingual foundations make the site accessible to a broader audience and adaptable for international communication work.

## Summary

The **Miriam Gradel** platform provides a polished, user-focused environment for exploring journalism, multimedia content, and communication services.  
With an emphasis on clarity, accessibility, and narrative structure, the site helps editors, readers, and clients discover Miriam‚Äôs work in a seamless and aesthetically cohesive experience.

# Planning

The planning phase for the **Miriam Gradel** platform began with a strong emphasis on **brand definition**.  
Working closely with **Marie Pilot**, we developed a cohesive visual and narrative identity for Miriam, that reflects her voice and personality as a journalist, her professionalism, and the clarity and precision of her communication work.

Rather than relying on formal user personas, the direction was shaped through a **brand-driven approach**: understanding Miriam‚Äôs audience, the publications she works with, the potential clients she will collaborate with, and the tone she brings to her reporting and communication strategies.  
This informed decisions on typography, layout, colour palette, the use of bespoke ribbons and animations, and the overall editorial feel of the website.

A core goal was to design a platform that felt **elegant, modern, and readable**, with an emphasis on:
- clean editorial layouts  
- intuitive mobile-first navigation  
- adaptable content structures for written, audio, and video journalism  
- a seamless bridge between journalism and communication services  
- a behind-the-scenes area as an opportunity for Miriam to introduce and explain her work and methods.

Responsive design was integral to the planning process. Since audiences frequently consume journalism on mobile devices, the entire site was built **mobile-first**, combining Bootstrap‚Äôs grid system with a fully custom design system. This ensures that the platform feels polished and consistent across all screen sizes ‚Äî desktop, tablet, and mobile.

## Agile Methodology

The project followed an **Agile, iterative workflow**, with ongoing refinement as new content, design adjustments, and technical components were introduced.  
Features were developed in cycles, tested, adjusted, and refined continuously throughout the project rather than in fixed waterfall stages.

To maintain clarity and structure, we applied the **MoSCoW prioritization framework**.

This ensured delivery of a stable, fully functional platform while allowing space for future enhancements as Miriam‚Äôs portfolio and communication work evolves.

# Residenza 126

To support the functionality of the **Residenza 126** website, the following models have been designed and implemented to store essential property, user, and contact-related information in the database.

The **User** table in the ER diagram serves as a conceptual representation only. It does not directly correspond to the models in `models.py` or the actual physical database tables but offers a logical view of how data entities relate to each other. The **User** model handles the management of registered users, including their personal information, preferences, and interactions with properties.

The **Property** and **UserProfile** models are designed to handle the details of the properties available for viewing or purchase and the user profiles, respectively. These models store critical details such as property name, features, location, and user preferences for liked properties.

Additionally, the **Contact** model captures the information submitted by users via the contact form on the website. This model allows users to reach out with inquiries about the properties or any other questions they may have regarding **Residenza 126**. The form data is stored securely, including fields such as name, email address, phone number, message, and the date/time of submission.

The **Entity Relationship Diagrams (ERD)** below illustrate how the models are interconnected, showcasing the relationships between users, properties, user profiles, and contact submissions. This ensures that all relevant data is properly organized and accessible for various website functionalities, such as property browsing, saving favorites, managing user profiles, and handling contact inquiries.

- UserProfile has a one-to-one relationship with User.
- UserProfile has a many-to-many relationship with Property through the liked_properties field.
- Property has a many-to-many relationship with User through the liked_by field.
- Property has one-to-many relationships with PropertyImage and PropertyVideo.
- Contact stands alone without direct relationships to the other models.

The visual identity for the **Miriam Gradel** platform was developed collaboratively with **Marie Pilot**, with the goal of creating a brand system that feels modern, editorial, and distinctively tied to Miriam‚Äôs journalistic style.  

Because the site focuses on journalism and communication, the visual language takes inspiration from **printed media** ‚Äî especially the look of a **highlighter drawn across a newspaper or book margin**. This concept shaped the signature ribbon elements, cyan fins, and bold, contrast-heavy layouts. Blue is a personal favourite of th client.

### Typography

The typography of the **Miriam Gradel** platform is designed to feel **editorial, modern, and highly readable**, mirroring the experience of a clean, professional publication.  
The system uses two core fonts:  
- **Designer** ‚Äî for expressive, statement-level headings  
- **TT Chocolates** ‚Äî for all body text, subheadings, menus, captions, and UI elements

Together they create a balance between **bold personality** and **professional clarity**, suitable for long-form journalism, multimedia storytelling, and communication work.

**Designer** is a geometric, futuristic display typeface that gives the platform a distinctive identity.  
It is not used for body content, but only for visual highlights where strong character is needed.

![Cinzel](staticfiles/README_docs/images/Cinzel.png "cinzel")

**TT Chocolates** is a clean, modern sans-serif with excellent readability across mobile and desktop.  
Its soft geometry and range of weights (from ExtraLight to ExtraBold) allow for a fully flexible typographic hierarchy.

TT Chocolates is particularly suited for an editorial website because it provides:

- **High legibility** for long articles  
- **Neutral tone** that doesn‚Äôt distract from journalistic content  
- **Consistent rhythm and balance** across different content areas  
- **Professional yet warm personality**, aligning with Miriam‚Äôs brand

![Cinzel](staticfiles/README_docs/images/Cinzel.png "cinzel")

The result is a typographic system that feels **journalistic, contemporary, and unmistakably Miriam Gradel**.

## Bugs

This section outlines key issues encountered during development, their underlying causes, and the solutions implemented.

#### 1. **Bug: Featured Player Metadata Not Updating After Media Swap (Audio & Video Pages)**

**Problem:**  
On both the **Video** and **Audio** pages, when a user selected an item from the ‚ÄúOther Examples‚Äù grid, the embedded media correctly loaded into the featured player, but **only the title updated**.  
Other metadata ‚Äî such as:
- description / standfirst
- publication date
- external link  
remained from the originally featured item or were cleared entirely.

This resulted in an inconsistent user experience where the player content and its contextual information did not match.

**Cause:**  
The initial implementation only swapped the **embedded media HTML** (`iframe`) and the **title field**.  
Key metadata (description, date, external URL) was:

- Rendered server-side for the first featured item only
- Not preserved in a reusable format
- Not stored in a way that could survive client-side swaps

Additionally:
- The description was rendered as rich text, making it unsafe to directly extract from the DOM
- Once replaced, there was no reference to restore the original featured content when swapping back

**Solution:**  

The fix was implemented consistently on **both the Video and Audio pages** using the same architecture.

**Key steps:**

1. **Metadata Caching via Data Attributes**  
   - All media cards now store their metadata (`title`, `date`, `description`, `external_url`) using `data-*` attributes.
   - Rich text descriptions are safely stored using escaped HTML (`escapejs`) to preserve formatting.

2. **Featured State Preservation**  
   - On initial page load, the original featured media‚Äôs:
     - embed HTML
     - metadata
   is cached in a hidden `<template>` and a hidden metadata store.
   - This allows the original featured item to be restored correctly when another item is promoted.

3. **Full Metadata Swap Logic**  
   - When a grid item is clicked:
     - The embedded media is swapped into the featured player
     - **All metadata fields** (title, date, description, link) are updated together
   - The previously featured item is demoted back into the clicked card with its full metadata restored.

4. **Shared, Reusable Logic**  
   - The same swap logic pattern is used for both **Video** and **Audio** pages.
   - This ensures consistent behavior and easier maintenance.

**Result:**  

- The featured player now **always displays the correct metadata** after a media swap.
- The original featured content is preserved and restored correctly.
- The interaction feels seamless and intuitive to the user.
- The solution is scalable, allowing editors to add unlimited media items without breaking layouts or behavior.
